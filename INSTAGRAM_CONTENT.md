## üìö Where to Find Studies (Evidence-Based Sources)

### **Top Sources for Physiotherapy Research:**

#### **1. PubMed (FREE - Best Overall)**
- URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
- Search: "lower back pain", "hip flexor tightness", "desk posture"
- Filter: Last 5 years, Human studies, Free full text
- **How to use:** Read abstract, grab key findings, cite in video

#### **2. Google Scholar (FREE)**
- URL: https://scholar.google.com/
- Finds everything PubMed finds + more
- See "Cited by" count (higher = more credible)
- Click "All versions" to find free PDFs

#### **3. The Physiotherapy Evidence Database (PEDro) (FREE)**
- URL: https://pedro.org.au/
- Quality-rated physiotherapy studies
- Easier to read than PubMed
- **Best for:** Treatment effectiveness, exercise interventions

#### **4. Cochrane Library (FREE)**
- URL: https://www.cochranelibrary.com/
- Systematic reviews & meta-analyses (highest quality evidence)
- Plain language summaries
- **Best for:** "What does ALL the research say?"

#### **5. ResearchGate (FREE)**
- URL: https://www.researchgate.net/
- Request full papers from authors directly
- Many researchers share PDFs for free

#### **6. Sports Medicine Journals**
- British Journal of Sports Medicine (BJSM)
- American Journal of Sports Medicine
- **Best for:** Athletic injuries, return to sport
